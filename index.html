Banks are so 90s! Say hello to FinTech.

So what makes FinTech companies different from traditional banks? Simple, they are built from the ground up with one and only goal. Make the movement of money from point A to point B faster, hassle free and readily available - at your fingertips!

In the process, they innovate and provide new services that relate to money. With many of these solutions you can now save, pay, invest, fund and do pretty much everything that would normally require a bank.

No wonder technology giants like Google, Apple, Facebook, Amazon and traditional banking giants like Goldman Sachs are all moving into the FinTech dancefloor.
In the meantime, these companies offer ground-breaking solutions that traditional banks simply cannot seem to keep pace with. For example, you can have accounts in multiple currencies, electronic IBANs, some of them even cater for transactions in cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin and Ethereum.

The most important point of all is that any company dealing with money, is fully regulated and licensed to operate as a payment institution. So rest assured, your money is safe.

So is this the end of banks? Well, yes and no. You see, many traditional banks are now evolving to meet the payment market needs for faster, simpler and flexible payment solutions. In the process, traditional banking powerhouses are buying out major FinTech providers to keep pace. As such, the value of FinTech companies is constantly on the rise. How much value you may ask? Well, the global FinTech sector is forecasted to be worth $305 Billion by 2025, a forecasted growth rate of 22% in 5 years!

Not too shabby now, is it?
